Types of Massage Sessions Offered

Swedish Massage

One of the primary goals of the Swedish massage technique is to relax the entire body. This soothing massage uses mild to moderate pressure with benefits, including relief from aches, pains, decreased stress, enhanced mental clarity, improved appearance, greater flexibility, & improved circulation. Swedish massage techniques include circular pressure applied by the hands and palms, firm kneading, percussion-like tapping, bending and stretching.

Deep Tissue Massage

Deep tissue massage therapy is similar to Swedish massage, but the deeper pressure is beneficial in releasing chronic muscle tension. This type relieves tension, knots and pain. It can be a full-body massage or just certain parts that need to be focused on, using a deeper pressure. Highly effective for stress areas due to misalignment, repetitive motions & past injuries. It's used for chronic aches and pain and contracted areas such as a stiff neck and upper back, low back pain, leg muscle tightness, and sore shoulders.

Sports Massage

This massage enhances performance by helping to prevent injury, reduces pain and swelling, relaxes the mind, increases flexibility, improves recovery rates, and promotes the body's natural immune function. This massage helps to prevent injuries, prepare the body for athletic activity and maintain it in optimal condition. It is designed to help athletes recover from workouts and injuries. Sports massage has three basic forms: pre-event massage, post-event massage, and maintenance massage. Sports massage therapy is geared toward athletes of every kind, from world-class professionals to weekend joggers. Focusing on areas of the body that are overused and stressed from repetitive and often aggressive movements.

Cupping Massage

Cupping or Myofascial decompression can loosen muscles and promote blood flow through tight tissue and muscles. It can also assist in treating pain and muscle spasms with the use of fixed or moving cups. Cupping therapy is an ancient form of alternative medicine. It serves many purposes, including to help with pain, inflammation, blood flow, relaxation and well-being, and as a type of deep-tissue massage.
